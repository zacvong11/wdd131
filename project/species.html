Utah's Native Cutthroat

Utah is home to four native cutthroat trout species, each with its own distinct habitat and native waters. The Bonneville cutthroat trout (Oncorhynchus clarkii utah) is perhaps the most well-known, inhabiting various cold, clear streams and lakes across the state, including the Weber, Provo, and Bear River drainages. The Yellowstone cutthroat trout (Oncorhynchus clarkii bouvieri) is found primarily in the Bear River drainage and some isolated streams in northeastern Utah. The Colorado River cutthroat trout (Oncorhynchus clarkii pleuriticus) is native to the Green River drainage in the northeastern part of the state, as well as the Colorado River and its tributaries in southern Utah. Lastly, the rare and federally threatened Lahontan cutthroat trout (Oncorhynchus clarkii henshawi) historically occupied several streams and lakes in the Bonneville Basin, including the Great Salt Lake, but its range has significantly diminished over time due to habitat loss and other factors. Efforts are ongoing to conserve and restore native cutthroat trout populations and their habitats throughout Utah..
