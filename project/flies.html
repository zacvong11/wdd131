Cutthroat Flies

The fly patterns you choose will vary depending on the species of cutthroat, time of year, and your personal preference. Generally speaking, in the warmer months flies will be hatching, bringing a lot of attention and activity to the surface of the water, making it the perfect time to throw a dry fly. That being said, regardless of the time of year, unless there is an active hatch going on, trout generally do 70% of their eating below the surface of the water. Again, this is an estimation, but a good rule of thumb is even when trout are eating off the surface, they're also eating below. Wet flies historically catch more fish than dry flies, but this will ultimatley come down to the angler.
